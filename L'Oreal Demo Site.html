Why this matters

Sarah is one of 46M+ loyalty members who drive 95% of L'Oréal sales. Diamond members visit 3x more often and spend 5x more than the average guest.

Her last session ended without a purchase. The in-session signal — mascara browsing, abandoned at PDP — is the most predictive data L'Oréal has, and it is only useful if it is reflected right now, not on tomorrow's email.

The opportunity: recognize the in-session intent, cross it with her Beauty Graph affinity, and surface a complete eye routine in the moment — not after the session ends.
Without Redis: Sarah sees the same generic home screen as everyone else. The mascara signal sits in a batch queue, she closes the app, and the moment is gone.
Stage 3 · Ingest

L'Oréal's data sources flow into Redis.

Redis Data Integration continuously syncs changes from Adobe CDP, the commerce platform, and the warehouse. FeatureForm orchestrates Beauty Graph features from Databricks. Batch profiles, transactional state, and live behavioral events converge in one low-latency layer.

Stage 8 · LTV optimization

Personalization meets lifetime value.

L'Oréal optimizes for guest lifetime value, not just this session's basket. Diamond members get a routine-completion boost, replenishment-overdue products get a recency multiplier, and the model balances immediate conversion against long-term loyalty health.

LTV-weighted ranking logic
Diamond tier LTV multiplier1.3× routine-completion
Replenishment overdue boost1.25× category-overdue
Birthday window boost1.2× gift-eligible
Frequency cap (same brand)Max 2 SKUs / home
Personalization quality floorNever below 75%
The key insight: a 3-product eye routine (mascara + palette + primer) at $92 AOV generates 2.5× more LTV than a single $26 mascara, because routine completion drives the next visit — and the visit after that.
